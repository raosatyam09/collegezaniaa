Most of the world’s schoolchildren still lack access to the minimum level of physical education, it says in the first-ever Global Status Report on Quality Physical Education.

The report, “Global State of Play”, by UNESCO’s education department found that 58 % of countries have included physical education for girls as mandatory only and that merely 7 % of the world’s schools have introduced boys’ and girls’ equal physical education time.
Two-thirds of secondary and over half of primary students around the world do not receive the minimum weekly physical education. A team expert informed PTI that fewer than one in two primary school teachers have had specialist training in physical education. The team also pointed out huge variations in the funding of physical education across countries – two-thirds spend less than 2 % of their education budget on it and one country in every 10 spends more than 7 %. “Physical Education (PE) has been the focus of widespread debate among education and health professionals globally because of its potential to enhance learning and contribute to students’ overall development. Professional development opportunities are limited because of lack of funds and/or lack of time in teachers’ schedules to participate in training.”. “For example, just 33 % of nations reported offering in-service training (INSET) and continuous professional development (CPD) to their PE workforce every five years, which is considerably different from the annual interval recommended,” the report added.
